Assessments & Educator Access

Every unit includes quizzes, a unit test, and answer keys. They're kept off the public site so students can't just search their way to the answers — verified educators get the whole collection.

How to get access

Request

Fill out the short request form using your school email address. A district or school domain makes verification fast.

Verify

I confirm you're an educator — usually by matching your email to a school's public staff directory. This is a human check, not a bot.

Access

You'll get a Google Drive folder shared to your school account with every assessment, key, and editable source file.

Open the request form

Form not working or no school email? Email from any address and include a link to your school's staff page.

Common questions

What's included with educator access?
Unit quizzes, unit tests, semester finals, answer keys, and editable source files (Google Docs/Forms) for every unit of both courses — Conceptual and Mathematical Chemistry. You can adapt everything to your own standards and pacing.
Why aren't assessments just posted publicly?
Because students are excellent at using search engines. Keeping tests and keys behind a quick verification step keeps them useful for every classroom that relies on them.
I'm a homeschool parent or tutor. Can I get access?
Yes — email and tell me a little about your situation. Verification just looks a bit different without a school directory.
Can I share the assessments with my department?
Please have each teacher request access individually — it's fast, and it means everyone gets updates and replacement versions directly.
How long does verification take?
Usually within a few days. Remember there's one chemistry teacher on the other end of this — probably grading lab notebooks.
